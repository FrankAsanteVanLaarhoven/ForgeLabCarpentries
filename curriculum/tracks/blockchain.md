# Track: Blockchain

**Audience:** builders interested in decentralized coordination, verifiable compute, and
incentives for autonomous agents.
**Prerequisites:** Foundations (Git, shell); some programming experience.
**Outcome:** read/write basic smart contracts and reason about when (and when not) to use a chain.

## Episodes

| # | Episode | Objectives (learner can…) | Status |
|---|---------|---------------------------|--------|
| 21 | What a Blockchain Actually Guarantees | explain consensus, immutability, and the real trade-offs vs a database | outline |
| 22 | Your First Smart Contract | write, test, and deploy a simple contract on a testnet | outline |
| 23 | Decentralized Coordination for Agents | use on-chain state for multi-agent agreements, escrow, or task markets | outline |
| 24 | Verifiable Compute & Provenance | anchor model/data provenance and attestations on-chain | outline |
| 25 | Security & Pitfalls | common vulnerabilities, audits, and "don't use a chain for this" | outline |

## Design notes

- Lead with **honest framing**: most problems don't need a blockchain. Teach learners to tell
  the difference — this credibility is the point.
- Always use **testnets / local chains**; never require real funds.
- Tie to the robotics/AI theme: provenance of training data, attestation of autonomous
  decisions, decentralized task allocation across a fleet.

## Suggested workshop slices

- **"Blockchain Reality Check" half-day:** Episodes 21–22.
- **"Decentralized Autonomy" 2 days:** Episodes 21–25.
