# Code of Conduct

ForgeLab Carpentries is dedicated to providing a welcoming, supportive, and harassment-free
experience for everyone — learners, helpers, instructors, maintainers, and organizers —
regardless of gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical
appearance, body size, race, age, religion, nationality, level of experience, or technology
choices.

This Code of Conduct applies to all ForgeLab spaces: workshops (in-person and online),
collaborative notes, chat channels, mailing lists, video calls, the issue tracker, and
pull-request discussions.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Showing patience with learners — everyone starts somewhere.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and for learning.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of workshops or other events.

## Live-coding etiquette

Because our model is participatory live coding, we ask everyone to additionally:

- Never make a learner feel slow or behind — pacing is the instructor's responsibility.
- Avoid "well, actually" interruptions during demos; save corrections for the helper channel.
- Treat every question as a good question; many learners share it silently.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the workshop's lead
instructor or to the Code of Conduct contact for the event. Reports can be made in person,
by direct message, or by email. All reports will be handled with discretion.

**Code of Conduct contact:** frankleroyvan@gmail.com

When reporting, please include:

- Your contact information (so we can follow up).
- Names (real or usernames) of those involved, and any witnesses.
- What happened, when, and where.
- Any available records (links, screenshots).

## Enforcement

Organizers are responsible for clarifying standards and will take appropriate, fair
corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate. Consequences may
range from a warning, to removal from an event, to a permanent ban from ForgeLab spaces.
