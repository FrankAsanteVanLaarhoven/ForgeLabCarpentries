# Governance

ForgeLab Carpentries is a volunteer-powered community with lightweight core coordination.
This document describes how decisions are made and how the project sustains itself.

## Roles in governance

- **Core team** — a small group responsible for overall direction, scheduling, and
  stewardship of the curriculum. Keeps coordination lightweight; delegates broadly.
- **Maintainers** — own one or more lessons/tracks; review PRs; keep content current.
- **Trainers** — run instructor training and certify new instructors.
- **Instructors & helpers** — deliver workshops.
- **Community members** — anyone learning, contributing, or attending.

See [community/roles-and-pathways.md](community/roles-and-pathways.md) for day-to-day duties.

## Decision making

- **Routine changes** (typos, exercises, clarifications): a maintainer reviews and merges.
- **Substantive changes** (new track, restructuring, policy): open an issue for discussion;
  seek rough consensus among maintainers and the core team before merging.
- **Disagreements**: default to discussion and consensus. If consensus stalls, the core team
  makes a timely call and records the rationale in the issue.

We value *timely, transparent* decisions over perfect ones — and we iterate.

## Sustainability

ForgeLab is designed to run on volunteer effort plus a thin layer of coordination. Funding
sources we pursue, in rough priority:

1. **Institutional partnerships / memberships** — universities, labs, robotics programs.
2. **Host fees** — organizations hosting custom or on-site workshops.
3. **Grants** — open-science, capacity-building, and STEM-education funding.
4. **Corporate custom training** — tailored cohorts for robotics/AI teams.

Surplus funds go to instructor recognition, accessibility, and infrastructure.

## Local hubs / chapters

Once a region has enough certified instructors, it may form a local hub to run workshops
under the ForgeLab banner, following this governance and the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Amending this document

Propose changes via pull request. Governance changes require core-team sign-off and a
two-week comment window.
